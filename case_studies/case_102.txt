=== CASE STUDY PROFILE ===
Topic: Anticipatory grief and adjustment difficulties in problem-solving

=== CLINICAL METADATA ===
Caregiver Profile: Adult child (age not specified), recently experienced loss of mother
Clinical Goal: Assist the client in identifying and articulating their problem-solving process to improve decision-making and conflict resolution skills, while acknowledging and addressing anticipatory grief.
Summary: "I'm struggling to make decisions and resolve conflicts after my mom's recent passing, and I feel lost and overwhelmed, unsure of how to get back on track."

=== PATIENT EMOTIONAL PROFILE ===
Primary Emotions: Overwhelmed, Lost, Sadness
Cognitive State: Catastrophizing, Flooding (due to recent loss)
Underlying Need: Emotional safety and control in the face of uncertainty

=== COMMUNICATION TECHNIQUES USED ===
Active listening, Validation, Reframing (by acknowledging commonality of grief-related problem-solving difficulties)

=== THE TRANSCRIPT ===
User: I've been struggling to understand my problem-solving process, and the steps I naturally go through. The recent passing of my mother has left me feeling lost and overwhelmed, and I've found myself unable to make decisions or resolve conflicts effectively.
Counselor: I'm truly sorry for your loss. It's not uncommon for individuals dealing with grief to experience difficulty in their problem-solving abilities. The problem-solving intervention we discussed can be a valuable tool in helping you navigate this challenging time. By articulating your thought process out loud, you'll be able to identify the specific steps you take when dealing with a problem, even if they occur quickly or over an extended period.
