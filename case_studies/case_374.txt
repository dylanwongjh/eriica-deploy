=== CASE STUDY PROFILE ===
Topic: Anticipatory grief and caregiver emotional processing

=== CLINICAL METADATA ===
Caregiver Profile: 55-year-old male spouse, caring for a terminally ill wife
Clinical Goal: To help the client process his anticipatory grief, manage feelings of guilt and sadness, and find a way to express himself authentically to his wife.
Summary: I've been caring for my terminally ill wife, but since her diagnosis, I've been consumed by feelings of guilt and sadness, wondering if I'm doing enough to support her.

=== PATIENT EMOTIONAL PROFILE ===
Primary Emotions: Guilt, Sadness, Helplessness
Cognitive State: Catastrophizing (assuming he has failed his wife), Flooding (overwhelmed by emotions)
Underlying Need: Emotional safety and control

=== COMMUNICATION TECHNIQUES USED ===
Active listening, Validation, Reframing (normalizing the client's feelings as a natural response to the situation), Empowerment (encouraging self-expression through writing), Problem-solving (suggesting alternative methods for processing emotions).

=== THE TRANSCRIPT ===
User: I've been grappling with feelings of guilt and sadness since my wife was diagnosed with a terminal illness. I've tried to be there for her, but I can't help feeling that I've failed her in some way. I've been unable to shake these feelings, and they've been affecting my relationship with my family. I've tried to write a letter to my wife, but I can't seem to find the right words to express how I feel.
Counselor: I'm sorry to hear that you're experiencing such intense emotions and that they're impacting your relationships with both your wife and your family... (rest of transcript)
