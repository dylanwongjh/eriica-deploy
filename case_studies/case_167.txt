=== CASE STUDY PROFILE ===
Topic: Anticipatory grief and bereavement

=== CLINICAL METADATA ===
Caregiver Profile: Husband of deceased wife (age not specified)
Clinical Goal: To help the client process his emotions, find meaning in the present, and move forward after the loss of his wife.
Summary: I'm struggling to come to terms with my wife's sudden death, feeling empty inside and wondering if I should be blaming myself for not being able to save her.

=== PATIENT EMOTIONAL PROFILE ===
Primary Emotions: Sadness, Guilt
Cognitive State: Catastrophizing (thinking about what could have been done differently), Rumination (dwelling on past events)
Underlying Need: Emotional safety and control

=== COMMUNICATION TECHNIQUES USED ===
Active listening, Validation, Reframing, Future-oriented goal-setting, Normalization of emotions, Empowerment through self-care and support-seeking.

=== THE TRANSCRIPT ===
User: I've been struggling with the idea that my wife, [Name], passed away too soon... I feel hollow inside, and I'm not sure if I should feel guilty for not being able to change the situation.
Counselor: I can see how deeply you're affected by [Name]'s passing... It's essential to acknowledge these emotions and allow yourself to grieve... Consider engaging in future-oriented activities as a way to cope with your feelings and find meaning in the present.
